BACKGROUND OF THE STUDY

E-Government refers to the application of Information and Communication Technologies (ICTs) to enhance public sector activities and operations.

  • Involves computerization of government processes and services
  • Aims to enhance efficiency, transparency, and responsiveness
  • Utilizes channels like government portals, e-governance identity schemes, and payment gateways

The Gambia has employed national development plans and ICT policies to pursue digitization of public services, but implementation is still in its nascent stage due to:

  • Infrastructural challenges
  • Institutional barriers
  • Socio-economic issues
2 / 15

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Despite the acknowledged potential of e-Government to transform public administration, The Gambia still grapples with effective adoption and implementation of digital strategies:

  • Majority of government services still provided through traditional manual channels
  • Low internet penetration, especially in rural areas
  • Inadequate ICT infrastructure
  • Lack of adequate IT expertise
  • Digital illiteracy among citizens
  • Absence of coordination among government agencies leading to fragmented systems

This research seeks to address these problems by examining how IT can be strategically exploited to enhance the design, implementation, and management of e-Government services.

STUDY OBJECTIVES

The primary objective: To examine the role of Information Technology towards e-Government service development in The Gambia.

Specific objectives:

  1. To examine the extent and status of e-Government service implementation in government ministries, departments, and agencies (MDAs) of The Gambia
  2. To identify the roles of Information Technology towards the development, deployment, and effectiveness of e-Government platforms
  3. To identify the technical, organizational, and social barriers to proper use of IT in public service delivery
  4. To propose realistic and feasible IT-driven strategies that can increase availability, quality, and dependability of government services

RESEARCH QUESTIONS

  1. What is the current status of Information Technology in e-Government services in The Gambia, and to what extent are they being used by citizens and businesses?
  2. To what extent has Information Technology supported planning and implementation of e-Government initiatives?
  3. What are the main technical, infrastructural, and institutional barriers to the implementation of IT in public service delivery?

CONCEPTUAL FRAMEWORK

E-Government refers to the intended use of ICTs for reengineering and modernizing public institutions and service delivery.

Key technologies included:

  • Internet platforms and mobile applications
  • Electronic document management systems
  • Cloud computing
  • Biometric and digital identity systems
  • Online service portals

The conceptual framework treats e-Government as a multi-layer system integrating:

  • Technology
  • Institutional change
  • Citizen engagement

Information Technology acts as both an enabler and driver of innovation in public service delivery.

THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK

Technology Acceptance Model (TAM)

Designed by Davis (1989) to explain how individuals accept and use technology.

Key drivers:

  • Perceived Usefulness: belief that technology will improve job performance
  • Perceived Ease of Use: belief that using the technology will be effortless

Helps identify attitudes of government employees and citizens towards web platforms.

Diffusion of Innovations Theory (DOI)

Developed by Rogers (1962) to describe how innovations spread across cultures and social systems.

Adoption stages:

  • Knowledge, persuasion, decision, implementation, confirmation

Adopter categories:

  • Innovators, early adopters, early majority, late majority, laggards

Explains how different strata of the population accept or reject e-Government services.

THE GAMBIAN CONTEXT

Current Initiatives

  • Government Wide Area Network (GWAN)
  • Computerization of National Identity System
  • National ICT for Development Policy

Key Challenges

  • Uneven internet accessibility (especially rural)
  • Poor user knowledge and e-readiness
  • Outdated ICT equipment
  • Poor system maintenance
  • Human resource capacity deficiency
  • Delayed policy enforcement
  • Weak inter-agency collaboration
  • Disjointed systems and duplication of effort

There is an urgent need to leverage IT more effectively to enable holistic and inclusive e-Government services in The Gambia.

ROLE OF IT IN E-GOVERNMENT

Information Technology is the enabler which underlies e-Government systems:

  • Data Management Systems: Organize information for services like healthcare, taxation, and civil registration
  • Network Infrastructure: Fiber-optic networks and wireless communications connecting government offices and citizens
  • Cybersecurity: Protection of sensitive data and digital identities to maintain citizen trust
  • User Interface Design: Mobile applications and websites that are easy to use, accessible, and in local languages

IT is not just an automation tool but a strategic asset for:

  • Redefining government-citizen interactions
  • Improving service delivery
  • Ensuring transparency and accountability
  • Increasing efficiency and reducing costs
  • Enhancing overall government quality
