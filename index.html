Projectile Motion Problem

A ball is thrown straight up into the air, and its height (in meters) after t seconds is given by the function:

h(t) = -5t² + 20t + 1

a) Find the velocity function of the ball.
b) What is the velocity after 2 seconds?
c) When does the ball reach its highest point?
d) What is the maximum height of the ball?

Part A: Find the velocity function
Given: h(t) = -5t² + 20t + 1
Velocity is the derivative of position with respect to time
v(t) = h'(t)
v(t) = h'(t) = d/dt[-5t² + 20t + 1]
v(t) = -5 · 2t + 20 · 1 + 0
Using power rule: d/dt(t^n) = n·t^(n-1)
v(t) = -10t + 20
Simplifying terms
Therefore, the velocity function is v(t) = -10t + 20
Part B: Find the velocity after 2 seconds
From Part A, we found v(t) = -10t + 20
To find velocity at t = 2, substitute t = 2 into v(t)
v(2) = -10(2) + 20
v(2) = -20 + 20
v(2) = 0
Therefore, the velocity after 2 seconds is 0 m/s
This means the ball momentarily stops moving at t = 2s (at its highest point)
Part C: When does the ball reach its highest point?
The ball reaches its highest point when velocity = 0
At the highest point, the ball momentarily stops before falling back down
From Part A: v(t) = -10t + 20
Set v(t) = 0 and solve for t
-10t + 20 = 0
-10t = -20
t = 2
Therefore, the ball reaches its highest point at t = 2 seconds
Part D: What is the maximum height of the ball?
From Part C, we found that the ball reaches its highest point at t = 2
To find the maximum height, substitute t = 2 into the height function
h(t) = -5t² + 20t + 1
h(2) = -5(2)² + 20(2) + 1
h(2) = -5(4) + 40 + 1
h(2) = -20 + 40 + 1
h(2) = 21
Therefore, the maximum height of the ball is 21 meters
