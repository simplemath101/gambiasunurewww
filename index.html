🔼 Bullish Continuation Patterns

These patterns suggest the uptrend will continue after a brief consolidation period.

BULLISH

1. Bullish Flag

A sharp upward move followed by a rectangular consolidation that slopes slightly downward. Breakout continues the uptrend.

BULLISH

2. Bullish Pennant

Similar to a flag but with converging trendlines forming a small symmetrical triangle after a strong upward move.

BULLISH

3. Ascending Triangle

Horizontal resistance line with rising support line. Indicates buyers becoming more aggressive, typically breaks upward.

BULLISH

4. Symmetrical Triangle (Upward Breakout)

Converging trendlines with equal slopes. In uptrend context, typically continues upward after consolidation.

BULLISH

5. Cup with Handle

U-shaped rounded bottom (cup) followed by a small downward drift (handle). Classic bullish continuation pattern.

BULLISH

6. Measured Move Up

Three distinct moves: initial rally, correction, then continuation rally of similar magnitude to first move.

BULLISH

7. Ascending Scallop

Series of rounded bottoms that gradually ascend, showing increasing buying pressure over time.

BULLISH

8. Rectangle (Bullish)

Price moves between parallel support and resistance. In uptrend, typically breaks upward to continue trend.

BULLISH

9. Three Rising Valleys

Three consecutive lows that form at progressively higher levels, showing strong support building.

BULLISH

10. Bullish Channel

Two parallel trendlines sloping upward. Price respects both support and resistance within channel.

🔽 Bearish Continuation Patterns

These patterns suggest the downtrend will continue after a temporary consolidation period.

BEARISH

1. Bearish Flag

Sharp downward move followed by rectangular consolidation sloping slightly upward before continuing down.

BEARISH

2. Bearish Pennant

Strong downward move followed by small converging triangle before breakdown continues the downtrend.

BEARISH

3. Descending Triangle

Horizontal support line with descending resistance. Shows sellers becoming more aggressive, breaks downward.

BEARISH

4. Symmetrical Triangle (Downward Breakout)

Converging trendlines in downtrend context. Consolidation typically resolves with downward continuation.

BEARISH

5. Inverted Cup with Handle

Inverted U-shape (cup) followed by small upward drift (handle). Bearish continuation after breakdown.

BEARISH

6. Measured Move Down

Initial decline, consolidation, then continuation decline of similar magnitude to first move down.

BEARISH

7. Descending Scallop

Series of rounded tops that gradually descend, showing increasing selling pressure over time.

BEARISH

8. Rectangle (Bearish)

Horizontal consolidation within downtrend. Typically breaks down to continue the bearish move.

BEARISH

9. Three Descending Peaks

Three consecutive highs forming at progressively lower levels, showing weakening resistance.

BEARISH

10. Bearish Channel

Two parallel trendlines sloping downward. Price respects channel boundaries during downtrend.

🔄 Bullish Reversal Patterns

These patterns signal a potential change from downtrend to uptrend.

REVERSAL

1. Double Bottom

Two distinct lows at approximately same level with moderate peak between. Classic reversal signaling support.

REVERSAL

2. Triple Bottom

Three lows at similar level with rallies between. Stronger reversal signal than double bottom.

REVERSAL

3. Inverted Head and Shoulders

Three lows with middle low (head) deeper than two shoulders. Very reliable bullish reversal pattern.

REVERSAL

4. Rounding Bottom

Smooth U-shaped curve forming over extended period. Gradual shift from bearish to bullish sentiment.

REVERSAL

5. Diamond Bottom

Rare pattern forming diamond shape at market bottom. Expands then contracts before upward breakout.

REVERSAL

6. Falling Wedge (Bullish)

Converging downward-sloping trendlines. Decreasing volume and range indicate exhaustion before reversal.

REVERSAL

7. Descending Broadening Wedge

Expanding price range with downward bias. Increased volatility often precedes bullish breakout.

REVERSAL

8. Bump and Run Reversal (Bottom)

Sharp decline (bump) followed by consolidation along support line before upward run begins.

REVERSAL

9. Adam and Eve Double Bottom

Sharp V-bottom (Adam) followed by rounded bottom (Eve). Combination pattern showing strong reversal.

REVERSAL

10. Complex Head and Shoulders Bottom

Multiple shoulders or heads at similar levels. More complex but still reliable bullish reversal.

🔄 Bearish Reversal Patterns

These patterns signal a potential change from uptrend to downtrend.

REVERSAL

1. Double Top

Two peaks at approximately same level with moderate trough between. Classic resistance rejection pattern.

REVERSAL

2. Triple Top

Three peaks at similar level with declines between. Strong resistance showing distribution phase.

REVERSAL

3. Head and Shoulders (Top)

Three peaks with middle peak (head) higher than two shoulders. Most reliable bearish reversal pattern.

REVERSAL

4. Rounding Top

Inverted U-shaped curve forming over time. Gradual shift from bullish to bearish sentiment.

REVERSAL

5. Diamond Top

Rare diamond-shaped pattern at market top. Expands then contracts before downward breakdown.

REVERSAL

6. Rising Wedge (Bearish)

Converging upward-sloping trendlines. Momentum slowing despite higher prices signals exhaustion.

REVERSAL

7. Ascending Broadening Wedge

Expanding price range with upward bias. Increasing volatility often precedes bearish breakdown.

REVERSAL

8. Bump and Run Reversal (Top)

Sharp rally (bump) followed by consolidation along resistance before downward run begins.

REVERSAL

9. Adam and Eve Double Top

Sharp peak (Adam) followed by rounded top (Eve). Combination showing distribution and reversal.

REVERSAL

10. Complex Head and Shoulders Top

Multiple shoulders or heads at similar levels. Complex but reliable bearish reversal formation.

⚖️ Neutral / Bilateral Patterns

These patterns can break out in either direction depending on market conditions.

NEUTRAL

1. Symmetrical Triangle

Converging trendlines with equal slopes. Can break either direction, typically continues prevailing trend.

NEUTRAL

2. Rectangle (Neutral)

Horizontal consolidation between support and resistance. Breakout direction determines next move.

NEUTRAL

3. Broadening Formation

Expanding price range with diverging trendlines. Increasing volatility signals indecision.

NEUTRAL

4. Megaphone Pattern

Similar to broadening formation. Expanding swings show extreme market uncertainty.

NEUTRAL

5. Diamond Formation

Price expands then contracts forming diamond shape. Can break either direction from apex.

NEUTRAL

6. Consolidation Zone

Sideways price action without clear pattern. Accumulation or distribution before next move.

🎵 Harmonic Patterns

Advanced patterns based on Fibonacci ratios and geometric price movements.

HARMONIC

1. Gartley Pattern

M or W shape with specific Fibonacci ratios (0.618 retracement). Bullish or bearish reversal signal.

HARMONIC

2. Bat Pattern

Similar to Gartley but with 0.886 retracement of XA leg. Strong reversal indicator.

HARMONIC

3. Butterfly Pattern

Extension pattern with D point beyond X. Uses 1.27 or 1.618 extension for reversal zone.

HARMONIC

4. Crab Pattern

Extreme harmonic pattern with 1.618 extension. Deepest retracement pattern with high accuracy.

HARMONIC

5. Shark Pattern

Unique pattern with 0.886 and 1.13 ratios. Earlier reversal signal than other harmonics.

HARMONIC

6. Cypher Pattern

Modern harmonic using 0.786 retracement. Less common but reliable when identified correctly.

🌊 Wave and Fractal Patterns

Complex patterns based on wave theory and market psychology cycles.

WAVE

1. Elliott Wave

Five-wave impulse followed by three-wave correction. Foundation of wave analysis theory.

WAVE

2. Wolfe Wave

Natural rhythm pattern with converging wedge lines. Predicts price target and timing.

WAVE

3. ABCD Pattern

Simple three-move pattern with equal legs. Uses Fibonacci 0.618 and 1.618 for targets.

🕯️ Candlestick-Based Patterns

Short-term patterns formed by one to three candlesticks, showing immediate sentiment shifts.

CANDLESTICK

1. Morning Star / Evening Star

Three-candle reversal: downtrend, small-body candle (star), then strong up candle (morning) or reverse (evening).

CANDLESTICK

2. Engulfing Pattern

Second candle completely engulfs first. Bullish engulfing at bottom, bearish at top signals reversal.

CANDLESTICK

3. Doji Reversal

Open and close at same level creating cross or plus sign. Shows indecision and potential reversal.

CANDLESTICK

4. Three White Soldiers / Three Black Crows

Three consecutive strong candles. White soldiers (bullish) or black crows (bearish) show momentum shift.

CANDLESTICK

5. Hammer / Hanging Man

Small body with long lower shadow. Hammer (bullish) at bottom, hanging man (bearish) at top.

CANDLESTICK

6. Shooting Star / Inverted Hammer

Small body with long upper shadow. Shooting star (bearish) at top, inverted hammer (bullish) at bottom.

📖 Conclusion

Mastering chart patterns is essential for successful technical analysis. These patterns represent the collective psychology of market participants and provide valuable insights into potential price movements.

Remember:

  • No pattern is 100% accurate - always use confirmation
  • Combine patterns with volume analysis and other indicators
  • Practice identifying patterns on historical charts
  • Always use proper risk management and stop-losses
  • Higher timeframes generally provide more reliable patterns
